Headers & Volleys

Players Required  -  3+
1 Goalkeeper, others outfield.

Objectives:

  • Outfield players attempt to score goals with either a header or a volley
  • Objective number of team goals should be set beforehand (3 recommended)
  • If this number is reached, the current goalkeeper must do a forfeit (such as running a lap of the field/hall) and return to the goal
  • If the keeper catches the ball or a shot goes over or wide, the last outfield player to touch it must go in goal and the count restarts

Rules:

  • No shots within six yards of the goal
  • Only full volleys count as a goal
  • Players are not allowed to set themselves up for a shot
  • Outfield players are not permitted to use their hands

Passing Through the Lines

Players Required  -  6-9 recommended
3 groups evenly split.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • Two sets of cones should be set up approx. 15 metres apart, as shown
  • Players should be split into three groups, one group being placed in the middle of the cones and the others at either end
  • The players at either end of the cones should work together, passing the ball past those in the middle
  • The players in the middle should attempt to intercept the passes as they travel across the middle area
  • If a player intercepts a pass, they replace the last player to touch the ball

Rules:

  • All passes must be along the floor
  • Passing players must not enter the middle area
  • Intercepting players must not enter the end zones
  • Cones should be distanced further apart after every 10 completed passes without an interception

Lay-off and Shot

Players Required  -  3+ (more than 5 recommended)
1 Goalkeeper, 1 lay-off man, others shooting.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • Lay-off man should be positioned centre of goal approx. 18 yards out
  • Shooting players should form a queue approx. 6 yards from the lay-off man, as shown
  • Shooting players should take it in turns to pass the ball to the lay-off man
  • The lay-off man will lay the ball off either left or right
  • The shooting player will run onto the ball, striking it towards goal with either their left of right foot
  • Shooting players should retrieve their ball and return to the back of the queue for another turn

Rules:

  • Shooting players may call which side they would like the lay-off pass
  • Ball can be thrown instead of passed as a variation to incorporate the volleying technique
  • Lay-off man can be positioned closer or further from goal dependent on age group/ability

Keep Ball

Players Required  -  5+ (the more the better)
1 interceptor (dependent on number of players), others passing.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • Passing players should form a circle, as shown (the smaller the circle the higher degree of difficulty for passing players)
  • Intercepting player(s) should position themselves within the circle
  • Passing players should attempt to keep possession of the ball by passing between one another
  • Intercepting player(s) should attempt to gain possession of the ball either by intercepting passes or tackling
  • Passing players will gain 1 point for every pass to a player directly beside them and 2 for every other pass
  • If passing players reach a set number of points (20 recommended) without an interception, the circle should be reduced in size
  • If an interceptor gains possession, they should swap with the last passing player to touch the ball and the count will be restarted

Rules:

  • All passes must be below chest height
  • If a pass is played over chest height, the player who played it must choose an interceptor to swap with
  • Players should attempt to gain the correct balance of passers and interceptors, 4:1 being a recommended ratio

Football Tennis

Players Required  -  4+ (even numbers ideal)
2 teams of equal numbers.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • A tennis court type setting should be assembled, with a divider (ideally a net) and boundaries
  • Teams should position themselves either side of the divider
  • The rules are similar to that of a tennis match where players attempt to pass the ball over the divider, within the boundaries on the other side
  • If the ball bounces more than once on the other side, the team that kicked it will receive a point
  • If the ball bounces outside of the boundaries, the receiving team will receive a point
  • If the ball hits the net the receiving team will receive a point
  • An objective number of points should be set before the beginning of the game, with the team that reaches this number first winning the game

Rules:

  • Players may use any part of their body other than their hands to control/pass the ball
  • Players on the same team may pass the ball between one another, as long as the ball only bounces a maximum of once before being returned over the divider
  • Individual players may take as many touches as they like, as long as the ball does not bounce in between these touches
  • The amount of bounces allowed can be increased for those of a younger age or lower ability

H-O-R-S-E

Players Required  -  2+
All players play against one another.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • Players must decide upon an order in which to shoot the ball
  • The first player should attempt to make a shot from anywhere on the court using any style they wish (2 attempts)
  • If the shot is made, the other players will have three attempts each to replicate the shot (same place, same style)
  • If a player misses all three attempts at replicating a shot they will receive a letter (H-O-R-S-E)
  • If the first player misses both their attempts at setting a shot for the others to copy, the next player will attempt to 'set a shot'
  • Players who fail to 'set a shot' when it is their turn to do so, will also receive a letter
  • Players who receive all letters to spell the word HORSE are eliminated, last man standing wins!

Rules:

  • Individual players may not set the exact same shot more than once
  • A player who successfully 'sets a shot' will continue to do so until they fail (or pass this responsibility on to another player)
  • Players may only use their hands to shoot the ball, but can do such things as stand on one leg, lie down or face backwards
  • Although H-O-R-S-E is the generic name for this game, any word may be used

Killer

Players Required  -  3+
All players play against one another.
Requires 2 basketballs.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • Players should form a line behind the 3-point arc (or a similar distance if not on a generic court)
  • The first player in the line should attempt a shot at the basket, at which point the second player may begin shooting
  • Both players should continue shooting until they make a basket
  • If the first player scores before the second, they should pass their ball to the next player in the queue who may then begin shooting
  • If at any point the player who started shooting the latter of the two shooters scores first, the other player will be eliminated and play will restart
  • If there are only two players left, the first player should return to the 3-point arc after scoring, before shooting again
  • Players continue until the last player is eliminated, revealing a winner

Rules:

  • A players first shot should always be from behind the 3-point line
  • All shots thereafter should be from the spot the ball is retrieved
  • Only minor dribbling is permitted and no traveling or double-dribbling
  • Players may not intentionally interfere with one another, but accidental clashes of shots will invariably happen

Spot-up Shooting from the Arc

Players Required  -  3+
1 shooter, 1 passer and 1 rebounder (all players work together and switch).
Numerous basketballs would be useful.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • Players postion themselves as shown above
  • The player straight on behind the arc (red) is the shooter, the one to the side (blue) is the passer and the one beneath the basket the rebounder
  • The passer should pass a ball to the 'spot-up' shooter who shoots at the basket
  • The rebounder will catch the ball and pass it back to the passer to begin again
  • More than one ball would allow this to happen at a quicker pace
  • If there is more than one shooter they can either form a line, or 'spot-up' at different positions along the 3-point line
  • This drill should get players more accustomed to the catch and shoot motion of a spot-up shooter

Rules:

  • Shooters should catch and shoot the ball instantaneously
  • Players should switch places after a decided number of shots
  • A defender can be incorporated to 'hassle' the shooter and a second rebounder to contest for rebounds

Passing Race

Players Required  -  9+ (a number divisible by 3 is ideal)
3 teams evenly split.
Requires 3 basketballs.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • A start line and an end line should be established, roughly the length of a basketball court
  • Players should position themselves at one end of court, with their backs turned to the opposite end
  • The distance between players should be close enough that they can receive a pass from the member in front
  • This is a race so on a specific mark, the first player in the line should pass the ball over their head to the person behind
  • This player should then run to the back of the line as shown, whilst the second player passes the ball through their legs to the third member
  • The players will continue to alternate passes under and over, whilst running to the back of the queue
  • The first team to get all members across the end line wins!

Rules:

  • If the ball hits the floor, the team must restart from the start line

Steal the Dribble

Players Required  -  5+
1 stealer (dependent on number of players), others are dribblers.
Requires numerous basketballs.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • A start line and an end line should be established, roughly the width of a basketball court
  • All dribblers should position themselves behind the start line with a ball, as shown
  • The stealer(s) should be positioned in-between the start and end lines without a ball
  • On a specific mark, dribblers should attempt to dribble from the start to the end line
  • The objective of the stealer(s) is to steal as many balls from the dribblers as possible
  • Any dribblers who get their ball stolen will also become a stealer for the next round
  • The last dribbler standing wins!

Rules:

  • No traveling or double-dribbling is permitted
  • Dribblers should attempt to use both hands
  • Stealers should not foul intentionally when trying to get the balls
  • Ideally the game should begin with 1 stealer for every 4 dribblers

Precise Throwing

Players Required  -  1+
Training drill for Quarterbacks.
Numerous footballs and a target needed.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • A target with a reasonably sized hole (tire, hula hoop, etc.) should be hung roughly 10 feet from the ground
  • The player(s) should position themselves at a reasonable distance from the target
  • The player should attempt to throw footballs through the target
  • For every five successful attempts in a row the player should move 5 feet further from the target
  • This should be continued until this feat is deemed unachievable
  • This drill should dramatically improve passing accuracy over time

Rules:

  • To increase difficulty the player can also position themselves at a variety of angles from the target
  • To increase the difficulty further, another player/coach could move the target slowly

Tipped Catch

Players Required  -  2+
1 throwing, others catching.
Training drill for Receivers and Defensive Backs.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • Drill designed to help with catching tipped or inaccurate passes
  • The two players should line up facing one another approx. 15-20 yards apart
  • The player catching should begin running towards the thrower
  • At this point, the player throwing should release the ball intentionally high of the receiving player
  • The receiving player should attempt to tip the ball into the air and make the catch however possible
  • This drill should improve the awareness of Receivers and Defensive Backs to tipped or deflected passes

Rules:

  • The player throwing should attempt to place the ball too high to be caught first time, as this defeats the object of the drill
  • Receivers may use any means necessary to complete the catch
  • Ball should be thrown intentionally wide of the target on occasions if too easy

Changing Direction at Speed

Players Required  -  1+
Training drill for Running Backs.
4 markers required (cones, flags, tires, etc.).

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • 4 corners of a rectangle/square should be marked out, as shown
  • The player(s) should start by one of the markers and run the route provided
  • As the player(s) approach each marker they should drop the hand closest to it to get around it
  • Players should start slowly and gradually increase the speed as they get accustomed to the movements required
  • This drill will help to improve balance and quickness when running through traffic

Rules:

  • The ball should always be placed in the arm away from the marker
  • If more than one player, the next player should begin when the one before finishes
  • Player(s) should attempt to keep their body as close to the markers as possible as they go around them

Reaction Footwork

Players Required  -  2+
Training drill for Quarterbacks.
1 Quarterback, other is the caller.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • 3 'pockets' should be set up as shown, no more than 4 feet wide
  • Both the Quarterback and the caller/coach should assign numbers to each pocket before starting
  • The Quarterback (blue) should position themself as shown, with the coach (red) standing so they can be seen
  • The Quarterback will pretend to take a snap and drop back behind the pockets
  • The coach will precede to hold up either one, two or three fingers to represent a respective pocket
  • The Quarterback should react to these hand signals and move up as they would do into the pocket, then drop back
  • This should be continued for a number of minutes, then Quarterbacks should switch
  • This should improve footwork in and around the pocket and also reactions within a game situation

Rules:

  • The Quarterback should always have their head up
  • If no signal is given the Quarterback should continue to move sidewards behind the pockets
  • No words should be spoken, everything is visual
  • Difficulty can be increased by adding extra pockets

Distraction Drill

Players Required  -  4+
1 Quarterback, 1 Receiver (dependent on number of players), 2 defenders.
Training drill for Receivers.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • The Quarterback and Receiver(s) should stand facing each other approx. 15-20 yards apart
  • The defenders should position themselves in between the two with their hands in the air
  • The Quarterback should attempt to the throw the ball between the arms of the defenders to the Receiver(s)
  • The Receiver(s) must attempt to catch the ball, avoiding the distraction of the defenders
  • This drill will help to improve the catching of your Receiver(s) in traffic and also their concentration

Rules:

  • The Quarterback should attempt to deliver the ball at different angles and trajectories
  • The defenders should move their arms to help distract the Receiver(s)
  • To increase the difficulty add more defenders, or have the Receiver(s) turn their back before the ball is thrown

Pass Reaction

Players Required  -  4+
3 feeders, 1 central focus.
At least 2 balls needed.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • 3 players should position themsleves in a row with roughly 3 metres between each, as shown
  • 2 of these should have a ball and the other should not
  • The fouth player (the central focus) should stand approx. 5 metres from these, as shown
  • This player also does not have a ball
  • One of the two players with a ball should pass to the central focus on the other side, who will pass on to the player without a ball
  • As the central focus releases this pass, the other player who started with a ball should pass to him
  • The drill continues like this until players are ready to switch
  • This drill should help to improve reaction times and pass handling

Rules:

  • To increase difficulty the central focus could move laterally or vertically whilst receiving the passes
  • All passes should aim to be on target
  • More players can be added to further increase the difficulty

Passing Under Pressure

Players Required  -  12+ recommended.
2 teams evenly split (1 attack, 1 defence).

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • The attacking team (red) should line up staggered, whilst the defence (blue) should line up opposite, as shown
  • The attackers should attempt to quickly pass the ball along the line
  • The defence should move towards the attackers and 'pressure' them without tackling
  • Once the attackers reach the end of the line the drill is over and should be restarted
  • Attackers should attempt to go as quickly as possible without making mistakes
  • When the drill has been ran several times, the teams should switch roles
  • This drill will improve the teams passing in pressure situations

Rules:

  • To increase difficulty, the attackers can be moved back slightly and run onto the passes
  • Attackers should be timed to allow teams to compete against one another

4-way Lineout Practice

Players Required  -  12
4 teams (each with 1 jumper and 2 lifters).

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • 4 teams should be set up in a square as shown
  • Each team has 2 lifters (yellow) and 1 jumper (blue)
  • One jumper has the ball to begin and is lifted into the air
  • The second jumper will then rise and receive a pass from the jumper with the ball
  • This should be continued around the square for a number of repetitions
  • This drill should dramatically improve lineout technique

Rules:

  • Coaches should look for proper technique from both lifters and jumpers
  • To increase the difficulty for those of higher ability, two balls could be introduced
  • Teams should be set up at a fair distance from one another
  • As a variation the coach (red) may stand in the middle of the square and act as a scrum-half, receiving the ball from each jumper and passing it to the next

Kicking Accuracy Game

Players Required  -  5+
4 kickers, 1 interceptor.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • A square approx. 25 yards in height and width should be set up, as shown
  • Kickers (red) should position themselves at each corner of the square, one of which has a ball
  • The interceptor(s) (blue) should position themselves in the middle of the square
  • The kickers should work specifically on their Grubber and Drop kicks, passing to one another around the square
  • The interceptor(s) should attempt to catch the ball whilst they do this
  • Once an interceptor catches the ball they should switch places with the player who kicked it

Rules:

  • The ball should not leave the square, if it does the player who kicked it should switch with the interceptor
  • No set direction, kicks can go to and from any player
  • To increase the difficulty, increase the size of the square or add more interceptors
  • Coaches should look for good kicking technique

Ball Retention

Players Required  -  2+
Even numbers required (2 per group, 1 attacker and 1 defender).
1 ball needed per pair.

Set-Up and Objectives:

  • Attackers (yellow) and defenders (blue) should line up facing each other, as shown
  • Attackers will each have a ball in their hands
  • Both players should approach one another and meet in the middle
  • The attacker should turn back towards the way they came from (representing their own team) and attempt to stay facing this way
  • The objective of the defender is to turn the attacker, making him face the direction they came from (away from support)
  • This drill will work on both ball retention of attackers and defenders ability to stop this retention

Rules:

  • Both players should attempt to stay on their feet at all times
  • Coaches should look for attacking players to keep their body between the defender and the ball
